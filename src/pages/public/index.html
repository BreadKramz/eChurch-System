Discover the heart and soul of Our Mother of Perpetual Help Redemptorist Church

A Legacy of Faith

Our Mother of Perpetual Help Redemptorist Church has been a beacon of faith and community in Dumaguete City since 1974. Founded by the Redemptorist Fathers, our parish has grown from a small congregation to a vibrant community of believers committed to living out the Gospel message.

Named after Our Lady of Perpetual Help, our patroness, the church serves as a spiritual home for thousands of families, providing sacraments, education, and charitable works that reflect Christ's love for all people.

"We are called to be a community of faith, hope, and love, serving God and our neighbors with compassion and dedication."

Our Sacramental Services

Baptism

The first sacrament of initiation where a person is cleansed of original sin and becomes a member of the Church.

Confirmation

Strengthens one’s faith through the Holy Spirit, completing the process of Christian initiation.

First Holy Communion

The first time a baptized person receives the Body and Blood of Christ in the Eucharist.

Marriage / Matrimony

A sacred union between a man and a woman, blessed by God and witnessed by the Church.

Anointing of the Sick

A sacrament that offers spiritual healing and comfort to those who are seriously ill or near death.

Funeral Rites / Burial Masses

Prayers and Mass offered for the deceased, asking for their eternal rest and comforting the bereaved.

Church Certificates

Confirmation

A sacrament where baptized Catholics receive the gifts of the Holy Spirit, usually administered by the bishop.

Mass Offering

A donation made when requesting a priest to celebrate Mass for a specific intention (e.g., thanksgiving, healing, repose of a soul).

Funeral

The Catholic rites for the deceased, which include vigil prayers, funeral Mass, and committal at the cemetery.

Mass Card

A card given when a Mass offering is made, often sent to families as spiritual support (e.g., for a deceased loved one).

Sick Call

When priests visit the sick or elderly, offering confession, communion, and anointing for healing and spiritual strength.
